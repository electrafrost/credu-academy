FAQ

Practical questions

Can I continue to work for my employer or run my practice? ‚ñº
Yes. Participants are expected to remain professionally active. The environment is designed to support uninterrupted remote work with high-speed internet and proximity to Asia-Pacific time zones. The residency does not require you to pause your income.
What does a typical day look like? ‚ñº
There is structure without rigidity: dedicated build blocks during weekdays, a weekly cohort working session, and one-to-one mentoring. Evenings and weekends are flexible for your own professional work or study. The residency is paced for sustained execution, not burnout.
What is the accommodation like? ‚ñº
Serviced apartments within Forest City, shared with other residents. Kitchen facilities and living spaces included. Meals are provided daily. The focus is on removing logistical friction rather than hotel-style luxury.
Who is this NOT for? ‚ñº
This residency is not for those seeking passive instruction, a holiday, or a speculative trading programme. It requires autonomy, discipline and a willingness to learn new things and experiment.
Do I need to be a coder? ‚ñº
No. AI-assisted development tools allow professionals to build functional systems without a traditional computer science background. We value professional judgement and domain expertise over prior syntax knowledge.
Is this CPD accredited? ‚ñº
CPD hours are self-assessed by participants and logged throughout the programme. Most professional accounting bodies recognise structured technical development and relevant mentoring. We recommend self-assessing against your specific body's framework. Hours are recorded on blockchain as part of CREDU's credentialing proof-of-concept that you are participating in (early mover!).
Professional standing is no longer secured by credentials and passive adoption alone. Your credentials got you this far. What you can actually build and deploy is what takes you forward. CREDU gives you three months to do the work instead of just talking about it.
